UK HOUSE PRICES PROJECT

RESEARCH QUESTION

`Priced-out UK house-hunters turn to lorry-sized tiny homes` from a recent Guardian article.  `Cost of living: Rents and house prices continue to rise` a BBC article

`House prices drop at the fastest rate since the financial crash in 2008 - A typical property is now nearly GBP 7,000 cheaper`. from a sky news column

As a person who currently lives in a rented studio and plans to buy a property in the future, I became intrigued by these topics. So I decided to investigate the topic of rent prices and property prices in the UK.

Main Question: What are main the trends in rent prices and housing prices? What is its effect on affordability?

Sub Questions:

A, How much did the rent prices go up in recent years? Especially in England and London

B, How affordability is private rental housing in England?

C, What are the trends in property prices in the UK? Which region shows the most increase?

D, How does the price change affect affordability?

DATA ACQUISITION

The following datasets are used for this project.

1.    Private rental market summary statistics in England, Release date: 14 December 2022

https://www.ons.gov.uk/peoplepopulationandcommunity/housing/datasets/privaterentalmarketsummarystatisticsinengland

Monthly rental prices in England, calculated using data from the Valuation Office Agency and the Office for National Statistics, by bedroom category, region, and administrative area.

2.    Private rental affordability, England, Wales and Northern Ireland: 2021, Release date: 19 December 2022

https://www.ons.gov.uk/peoplepopulationandcommunity/housing/datasets/privaterentalaffordabilityengland

Percentage of total monthly household income spent on private rent in England, by country and region, from the fiscal year ending in 2013 to 2021.

3.    UK House Price Index: data downloads October 2022, Published on 14 December 2022

https://www.gov.uk/government/statistical-data-sets/uk-house-price-index-data-downloads-october-2022

The UK House Price Index (UK HPI) measures changes in the value of residential properties in the United Kingdom. The UK HPI uses sales data from residential housing transactions, whether they are cash or mortgage. This dataset contains data since January 1995 in England and Wales, since January 2004 in Scotland, and since January 2005 in Northern Ireland Data is available at the national and regional levels, as well as counties, local governments, and boroughs in London.  The file contains the Average price, Average price by property type, Sales, Cash mortgage sales, Index etc. For this study Average price, Average price by property type and Index were used.

4.    House price to residence-based earnings ratio, Latest version Release date 23 March 2022

https://www.ons.gov.uk/peoplepopulationandcommunity/housing/datasets/ratioofhousepricetoresidencebasedearningslowerquartileandmedian

Affordability ratios are calculated by dividing house prices by gross annual earnings from a residence. Based on the median and lower quartiles of English and Welsh house prices and earnings.

DATA PREPARATION

The first dataset `Private rental market summary statistics in England` contains a lot of data but all of them were arranged in table format and the required data were easily extracted and saved in simple excel files which were input into a tableau for visualisation.

Similar to the first dataset the second dataset `Private rental affordability, England, Wales and Northern Ireland` was also cleaned and extracted. The Data interpreter function available in tableau was extremely helpful. This function help to clean excel files to a certain extent. It cleaned most of the data used in this project to a good standard. It properly identified the Header and data part which was then fed into for visualisations.

The third dataset was a huge CSV file. It contains data on average price, Average price by property type, Sales, Cash mortgage sales, Index etc from 1968 to 2022. For the study of average prices data from 2006 to 2022 was extracted using a simple filter function and saved into another excel file. Further filtering was done in the tableau software itself when required using the filter option available in it.

The fourth dataset also contained a lot of data, but in this film also these were arranged into tables. And were easily extracted into tables in England regions and London regions.

C.   UK PROPERTY INDEX

The UK property index measures the average change in the value of residential properties in the UK. It is usually based on a sample of real estate sales prices and is used to track changes in the housing market over time. The index can be used to track changes in property values across the UK, as well as changes in the values of various property types, such as detached houses, semi-detached houses, and flats or apartments.

The index values are typically expressed as a percentage change from a base period, such as the year's average property prices. For example, if the index value is 150, it means that the sample's average property value has increased by 50% since the baseline period. If the index value is 90, it means that the sample's average property value has decreased by 10% since the base period.

The property price index is significant because of its ability to provide a comprehensive and consistent picture of the housing market. It is a useful tool for understanding how property prices vary across regions.

The property price index can also be used to identify housing market trends and patterns, such as periods of rapid growth or decline, which can help inform investment decisions, policy decisions, and research.

It can also be used as a benchmark for evaluating the performance of various investments, such as real estate funds, and as a tool for forecasting future housing market trends. Overall, the property price index is an important tool for understanding the health of the housing market.

Various organisations, including the government and private companies, publish indexes. They use different methodologies, sampling, and data sources, so the results may differ slightly.

D.   PROPERTY AFFORDABILITY IN ENGLAND AND LONDON

The ability of a person or household to pay for a house is referred to as affordability. Affordability in housing is typically defined as the relationship between housing costs (such as rent or mortgage payments) and income.

When housing costs (including mortgage or rent payments, property taxes, and utilities) are less than a certain percentage of a household's income, usually 30% or less, housing is considered affordable. When housing costs exceed this limit, it is deemed unaffordable.

The median multiple, which is the median house price divided by the median gross annual household income, is used to calculate housing affordability.
